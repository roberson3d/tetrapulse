Description

We Found a Couch is a three player, single screen co-op game where you and your friends join forces to battle a swarm of zombies using the only weapon you have left: eggs. Every throw of your hard boiled eggs keeps the horde at bay as you work together to protect the couch, an ancient relaxation device for you and your friends to chill.

History

Although it's appearance has changed much since the beginning of this Train Jam, We Found a Couch was originally inspired by it's spiritual predicessor Chiken Strut and a love of local multiplayer games. We continued the development of the game as students throughout the train ride. A few moments before it ended, we successfully built the game to put on the web. Then, we were excited to invite all of the internet to try the demo of the game. We are probably not continuing development.

Features

  • Single screen, local multiplayer for three players
  • Balance the couch
  • A single enemy types
  • Collective movement
  • High second to second action
  • Art style that blends nature and technology

About The Amiable

Nice. Pleasent even.
A Chicago based independent game studio aimed at reinventing what it means to sit down and play games among friends and strangers alike. With our combined powers in break dancing, fashion design, acoustic guitar, and pole vaulting we banded together to make video games. We use multiplayer games as a tool for making personal connections, growing communities, and to bring the virtual closer to reality.
